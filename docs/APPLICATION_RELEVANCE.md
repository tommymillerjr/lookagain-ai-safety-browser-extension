# Application Relevance

This project is relevant to cybersecurity, AI safety, trust & safety, and fraud/scam prevention roles.

## Cyber Threat Investigation Relevance

LookAgain connects to cyber threat investigation through:

- Phishing and impersonation risk
- Suspicious login and credential collection flows
- Browser-level abuse signals
- Risk scoring and explainability
- User-facing intervention design
- Deception pattern analysis

## Fraud and Scams Relevance

LookAgain connects to fraud and scam safeguards through:

- Social engineering awareness
- Scam landing-page detection concepts
- Warning UX
- False-positive restraint
- User trust and safety design
- Policy-like thinking around when to intervene

## AI Safety Relevance

As AI-generated content becomes more persuasive, scam and phishing workflows may become harder for users to recognize. LookAgain explores how safety tooling can help users pause and review before trusting suspicious pages.

## Personal Motivation

The project reflects an interest in building practical, privacy-respecting safety tools for people who may be targeted by increasingly convincing online deception.
